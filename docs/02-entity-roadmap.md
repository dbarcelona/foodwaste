# Entity Roadmap: Nonprofit, Benefit Corporation, and B Corp

This roadmap is preparation material, not legal or tax advice. Before filing, review with a startup attorney, nonprofit attorney, CPA, or a clinic such as Berkeley Law / local pro bono counsel.

## Core clarification

“Nonprofit” and “B Corp” are different concepts.

- A **501(c)(3) nonprofit** is a tax-exempt charitable organization. It can receive tax-deductible charitable contributions after recognition, but cannot distribute profits to private owners.
- A **benefit corporation** is a for-profit legal entity with stakeholder/public-benefit obligations.
- A **Certified B Corporation** is a private certification from B Lab for qualifying companies that meet governance, social, environmental, transparency, and accountability standards.

## Recommended initial path

Because the current concept includes paid corporate services, potential venture/impact capital, and B Corp interest, the likely best fit is:

1. California benefit corporation
2. B Corp-aligned policies from day one
3. nonprofit recipient partnerships
4. possible fiscal sponsorship for charitable grants if needed

## Path A: California benefit corporation pursuing B Corp certification

### Why choose this

- You can charge companies directly.
- You can raise equity capital.
- You can build a logistics/technology business.
- You can still pursue grants that allow for-profit applicants.
- You can embed public benefit in governance.
- You can later pursue Certified B Corp status.

### Formation preparation checklist

- choose legal name
- search California Secretary of State name availability
- decide corporation type and tax election strategy with CPA
- draft Articles of Incorporation with benefit corporation language
- appoint registered agent
- file with California Secretary of State through bizfile
- create bylaws
- issue founder shares
- hold first board/shareholder approvals
- obtain EIN from IRS
- open business bank account
- register for California tax accounts as needed
- review local business license requirements
- purchase insurance
- set up accounting
- prepare annual benefit reporting process

### B Corp preparation checklist

- create B Impact Platform account
- complete self-assessment
- define impact business model
- adopt stakeholder governance language
- document worker policies
- document supplier/vendor standards
- document environmental metrics
- document customer/community impact metrics
- maintain board minutes and policies
- collect evidence for claims
- review current B Lab standards before submission

Source: B Lab describes Certified B Corps as companies meeting verified social, environmental, and governance standards. B Lab US/Canada also states California corporations are encouraged to adopt benefit corporation status to meet legal requirements.

## Path B: California nonprofit public benefit corporation + 501(c)(3)

### Why choose this

- You want charitable donations and grants to be the core model.
- You want to issue donation receipts.
- You do not need venture equity.
- The primary mission is feeding people, not selling a logistics product.

### Formation preparation checklist

- choose nonprofit legal name
- recruit initial board directors
- define charitable purpose
- draft Articles of Incorporation for California nonprofit public benefit corporation
- include required 501(c)(3) purpose and dissolution language
- file Articles with California Secretary of State
- obtain EIN
- draft bylaws and conflict-of-interest policy
- hold organizational board meeting
- file initial Statement of Information within required state timing
- apply for federal 501(c)(3) recognition using Form 1023 or 1023-EZ if eligible
- apply for California exemption using FTB 3500 or 3500A depending on federal status
- register with California Attorney General Registry of Charities and Fundraisers if applicable
- set up accounting and donor receipt process
- prepare annual IRS Form 990 series and California filings

Sources:

- California Secretary of State provides nonprofit initial filing forms through bizfile.
- IRS says organizations seeking 501(c)(3) recognition generally file Form 1023 or 1023-EZ electronically through Pay.gov.
- FTB says Form 3500 is used to apply for California tax-exempt status; Form 3500A is for organizations with a federal determination letter.

## Path C: Fiscal sponsorship first

### Why choose this

- You want to test charitable grants/donations before forming a nonprofit.
- You want a 501(c)(3) partner to receive restricted funds.
- You need speed.

### What to validate

- sponsor fee
- who owns funds and assets
- whether food recovery operations are covered
- insurance coverage
- who signs donor/recipient agreements
- whether corporate paid services are allowed
- whether the sponsor can issue receipts for donations supporting the project

## Decision recommendation

Do not form both a nonprofit and for-profit immediately unless an attorney recommends it and you have a clear reason. Dual-entity structures create complexity around control, conflicts, pricing, grants, intellectual property, and private benefit.

Start with the benefit corporation path if paid pilots are the main path. Use nonprofit partners for distribution. Revisit nonprofit formation only if grant funding or charitable fundraising becomes the dominant strategy.

## Must-not-do list

- Do not promise tax-deductible receipts unless a qualified 501(c)(3) or fiscal sponsor is receiving the donation.
- Do not claim B Corp certification before certification is complete.
- Do not imply SB 1383 compliance unless agreements and records meet applicable requirements.
- Do not accept unsafe food to preserve a donor relationship.
- Do not let donors use the service as disposal for inedible food.
