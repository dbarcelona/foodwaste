# Market Research

## Research goal

Identify comparable food recovery operators, local recipient partners, and strategic gaps for an Oakland-first corporate catering recovery service.

## Summary

The Bay Area already has strong food banks, pantry networks, and food rescue organizations. The opportunity is not to claim no one is doing food recovery. The opportunity is to focus tightly on corporate catered surplus and make the donor-side workflow safer, easier, and more measurable.

## Regulatory and policy sources

### CalRecycle SB 1383 food recovery

Sources:

- https://calrecycle.ca.gov/organics/slcp/foodrecovery/donors/
- https://calrecycle.ca.gov/organics/slcp/faq/foodrecovery/

Important points:

- commercial edible food generators may need written agreements with food recovery organizations or services
- CalRecycle says cost-sharing provisions can be negotiated
- donors and recovery services should avoid donation dumping by defining acceptable food in agreements
- records can include parties, food types, frequency, and monthly pounds

### Liability protection

Sources:

- Federal statute: https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/42/1791
- USDA overview: https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/news/blog/good-samaritan-act-provides-liability-protection-food-donations
- California AB 1219 text: https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billTextClient.xhtml?bill_id=201720180AB1219

Important points:

- federal protection applies to good-faith donations of apparently wholesome food when statutory requirements are met
- protections do not eliminate the need to follow federal, state, and local food safety rules
- California expanded protections, but operational legal review is still needed

## Strategic gaps to test

1. Are Oakland companies willing to pay for food recovery without SB 1383 pressure?
2. Do caterers want a third-party recovery partner or prefer to own the process?
3. Which recipient organizations can reliably accept prepared food from corporate events?
4. What food types are most useful to partners?
5. What pickup windows are operationally realistic?
6. What insurance requirements do corporate donors impose?
7. How much documentation is enough for companies without creating pickup friction?
