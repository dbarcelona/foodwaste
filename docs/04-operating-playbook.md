# Operating Playbook

This playbook is a draft for pilots. It must be reviewed against local health department guidance, insurance requirements, and partner policies before live operations.

## Pickup eligibility

Accept only food that is:

- from a licensed commercial kitchen, caterer, grocery, office cafeteria, or permitted food facility
- apparently wholesome
- held and transported under safe conditions
- packaged or packageable for safe transport
- accepted by a recipient partner before pickup
- within the donor agreement's accepted food categories

Do not accept:

- homemade food
- food exposed to contamination
- food with unclear holding history
- food that recipient partners cannot use
- food with damaged/open packaging where safety is uncertain
- food the driver believes is unsafe
- food donated mainly as waste disposal

## Preferred food types for pilot

Start with lower-complexity categories:

- individually packaged meals
- unopened packaged salads or sides
- commercially prepared trays that remained under controlled service
- baked goods
- produce
- packaged snacks
- sealed beverages

Treat hot prepared foods and buffet-exposed foods as higher risk until protocols are reviewed.

## Pickup workflow

1. Donor submits pickup request.
2. Coordinator screens food type, amount, location, pickup window, and holding conditions.
3. Coordinator confirms recipient capacity.
4. Donor receives staging instructions.
5. Driver arrives and confirms:
   - donor contact
   - food type
   - packaging
   - visible condition
   - temperature where required
   - approximate quantity
6. Driver refuses ineligible food.
7. Driver logs pickup details.
8. Driver delivers directly to recipient.
9. Recipient confirms usable food received.
10. Coordinator sends donor confirmation.

## Data to collect

For every pickup:

- donor name
- donor contact
- caterer if applicable
- pickup address
- recipient organization
- pickup date and time
- delivery date and time
- food categories
- approximate quantity by tray, package, or pound
- temperature log when applicable
- driver name
- recipient confirmation
- refused items and reason
- photos only if allowed by donor and recipient

## Food safety equipment

Minimum pilot kit:

- calibrated probe thermometer
- infrared thermometer for screening only, not final internal temperature
- insulated bags or carriers
- clean bins
- gloves
- labels
- permanent markers
- sanitizer wipes
- pickup log sheets or mobile form
- scale or weight estimation process

## Donor agreement provisions to draft for counsel

- parties and contact information
- accepted food categories
- rejected food categories
- donor responsibility before pickup
- recovery service responsibility after transfer
- pickup windows
- cancellation policy
- cost-sharing or service fees
- documentation and records
- donor warranties limited to good-faith handling
- refusal rights
- insurance requirements
- incident process
- no tax receipt unless provided by qualified nonprofit/fiscal sponsor

## Recipient agreement provisions to draft for counsel

- accepted food categories
- delivery windows
- storage/handling responsibility after receipt
- confirmation process
- refusal rights
- capacity communication
- contact escalation
- records
- feedback process

## Insurance questions

Ask broker/attorney about:

- general liability
- product liability / foodborne illness coverage
- commercial auto or hired/non-owned auto
- workers compensation if employees
- volunteer accident coverage if volunteers
- directors and officers if incorporated
- cyber/privacy if storing donor/recipient data

## Pilot staffing

Keep the first pilot founder-led.

Roles:

- founder/operator: donor sales, partner relationships, QA
- dispatcher/coordinator: match donor to recipient
- driver: pickup and delivery
- recipient lead: confirms capacity and receipt

Avoid volunteer-only reliability for early corporate pilots. Volunteers can help later, but the first promise to companies should feel professional.

## Incident response

If unsafe food is discovered:

1. refuse or quarantine food
2. document reason
3. notify donor and recipient
4. do not redistribute
5. log incident
6. review SOP

If illness or complaint is reported:

1. stop related distribution
2. preserve records
3. notify insurance and counsel
4. follow health department guidance
5. communicate factually only
