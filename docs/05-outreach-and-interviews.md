# Outreach and Interview Kit

## Research objective

Validate whether companies, caterers, and recipient organizations want an Oakland-first corporate catering recovery service and what would make them trust it.

## Company interview questions

1. How often do you order catering for meetings, trainings, or events?
2. What usually happens to leftovers?
3. Who decides whether food can be donated?
4. What would make donation feel risky?
5. What would make donation feel easy?
6. Would impact reporting be useful internally?
7. Who would own budget for this: workplace, events, CSR, sustainability, procurement, or HR?
8. Would you prefer per-pickup pricing or a monthly plan?
9. What vendor requirements would we need to meet?
10. Would you pilot this for one event?

## Caterer interview questions

1. How often do clients ask about donating leftovers?
2. What food types are usually left after events?
3. What prevents donation now?
4. What pickup timing works with teardown?
5. What packaging issues come up?
6. What food safety concerns matter most?
7. Would this help you sell sustainable catering?
8. Would you prefer the client pays or the caterer bundles the service?
9. What instructions would staff need?
10. What would make you refuse to participate?

## Recipient partner interview questions

1. Do you accept prepared food from commercial kitchens?
2. What food types are most useful?
3. What food types are not useful?
4. What days and times can you receive food?
5. Can you accept same-day calls?
6. Do you need food packaged in specific portions?
7. Can you weigh or confirm received food?
8. What storage capacity do you have?
9. What would make a donor relationship burdensome?
10. What would make this genuinely helpful?

## Pilot one-pager copy

Food Recovery Oakland helps companies recover safe surplus food from catered events and redirect it to local food access partners.

Pilot includes:

- pre-event recovery plan
- caterer staging instructions
- pickup and delivery
- recipient confirmation
- impact report

Pilot asks:

- one event or recurring catered meal
- advance notice when possible
- designated donor contact
- food held and packaged according to agreed guidelines
- feedback after pickup

Pilot outcomes:

- pounds recovered
- estimated meals
- successful pickup timing
- recipient usefulness feedback
- operational lessons
