# Pitch Deck Outline

## Slide 1: Title

Share your food. Keep neighbors fed.

Oakland-first food recovery for corporate catering surplus.

## Slide 2: Founder story

Start with the personal arc:

- mom not allowing a single grain of rice to be wasted
- seeing food insecurity in NYC
- learning about food waste through media and research
- failed banana peel/onion skin water project as early reuse instinct
- volunteering at food pantries
- Berkeley/Oakland food insecurity
- corporate events with visible surplus

Takeaway:

This is not an abstract climate or hunger problem. It is a repeated handoff failure.

## Slide 3: Problem

Companies order large catered meals. Safe food remains. It is discarded because recovery is unclear, risky, and inconvenient.

## Slide 4: Why now

- Bay Area food insecurity remains visible.
- Companies need tangible local sustainability actions.
- California policy encourages edible food recovery.
- Existing nonprofits need logistics support, not just good intentions.
- Offices and events are returning with recurring catered moments.

## Slide 5: Solution

A pickup, safety, partner matching, and reporting service for corporate catered surplus.

Workflow:

Request -> screen -> match -> pickup -> deliver -> confirm -> report

## Slide 6: Market wedge

Not restaurants first. Not a discount marketplace.

Initial wedge:

- corporate events
- catered office meals
- event venues
- caterers
- Oakland-first, then San Francisco

## Slide 7: Business model

Revenue hypotheses:

- per-pickup fee
- monthly office recovery plan
- event recovery package
- equipment and signage kits
- grants for pilot infrastructure
- later software/reporting layer

## Slide 8: Competitive landscape

Comparable organizations:

- Replate
- Copia
- Food Runners / ExtraFood
- White Pony Express
- Waste No Food
- Food Recovery Network

Positioning:

Local operations plus corporate catering focus plus donor-side readiness and reporting.

## Slide 9: Pilot

First 90 days:

- 1 to 3 donor companies
- 1 to 2 caterers
- 2 to 4 recipient partners
- 5 to 10 pickups
- food safety logs
- partner interviews
- first impact report

## Slide 10: Ask

Depending on audience:

- companies: pilot one catered event
- caterers: become a launch partner
- nonprofits: advise recipient requirements
- funders: support pilot logistics
- advisors: legal, food safety, insurance, operations
