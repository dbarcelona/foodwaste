# Legal and Operations Prep Packet

This packet is for preparation only. It should be reviewed by a qualified attorney, CPA, insurance broker, and food safety advisor before use.

## Attorney questions

### Entity

1. Should the first entity be a California benefit corporation, social purpose corporation, LLC, or nonprofit public benefit corporation?
2. If the goal is Certified B Corp, is benefit corporation status the best initial structure?
3. What public benefit purpose language should appear in formation documents?
4. Should the company form as a corporation from day one if impact investors may participate?
5. Should a separate nonprofit ever be created, or should nonprofit partners/fiscal sponsorship handle charitable funds?

### Food donation and liability

1. How do federal and California food donation protections apply to a for-profit food recovery service?
2. Does the company need to be a nonprofit to receive liability protection under every scenario?
3. What contract language should donors and recipients sign?
4. What representations should donors make about food handling before pickup?
5. What should the recovery service disclaim or refuse to certify?

### Tax and donation receipts

1. Can a benefit corporation provide any donor documentation without implying tax deductibility?
2. What language should be used when donors ask for donation receipts?
3. Would fiscal sponsorship allow charitable contributions for pilot operations?
4. How should in-kind food value be handled?

### Employment and volunteers

1. Can volunteers perform pickups for a for-profit benefit corporation?
2. If yes, what waivers, insurance, and labor rules apply?
3. Should the pilot use contractors or employees for driving?

## Driver role description

Responsibilities:

- arrive during pickup window
- communicate with donor and recipient
- inspect food against acceptance checklist
- record temperature where required
- transport directly to recipient
- protect food during transit
- refuse unsafe or ineligible items
- complete logs

Requirements:

- valid driver's license
- clean driving record if required by insurer
- food handler training if required or recommended
- ability to lift filled containers
- professional communication
- no unauthorized stops with food in vehicle

## Refusal language

Food Recovery Oakland may refuse any item that appears unsafe, is outside the accepted categories, has unclear handling history, is not properly packaged, is not wanted by a recipient partner, or cannot be delivered within the required time window.

## Donation receipt language

Food Recovery Oakland is currently a startup concept and should not issue charitable tax receipts unless and until it is operating through a qualified nonprofit entity or fiscal sponsor. Donors should consult their own tax advisors regarding any food donation valuation or deduction.
