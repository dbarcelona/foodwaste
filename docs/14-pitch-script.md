# Pitch Scripts

## One-minute pitch

Food Recovery Oakland helps companies recover safe surplus food from catered meetings and events and deliver it to local food access partners.

The problem is not that companies hate donating food. The problem is that after an event, nobody owns the safe handoff. Event teams are busy, caterers are breaking down, recipient organizations need predictability, and companies worry about liability and logistics.

We solve the missing middle: intake, food safety screening, pickup, recipient matching, delivery confirmation, and impact reporting.

We are starting in Oakland with corporate catered surplus, then expanding to San Francisco once the pilot route is proven.

The first ask is simple: one company, one caterer, one recipient partner, and five pickups to prove the workflow.

## Three-minute pitch

Food waste and food insecurity often sit side by side. In corporate settings, companies order catering for meetings, trainings, and events. When food remains, it is often still edible, already paid for, and minutes away from being thrown out.

The obvious answer is donation. The operational reality is harder. Who confirms the food is eligible? Who calls a recipient? Who picks it up during event breakdown? Who documents the handoff? Who tells the company what impact it created?

Food Recovery Oakland is built for that missing middle.

We recover eligible surplus food from corporate events and catered office meals, then deliver it to local food access partners. We provide donor intake, caterer instructions, food safety logs, pickup and delivery, recipient confirmation, and impact reporting.

We are not starting as a restaurant discount app. That market is crowded and consumer-oriented. We are starting with corporate catered surplus, where the food is high-volume, often predictable, and where companies have budget for sustainability, employee engagement, and community impact.

The business model begins with paid pilots: per-pickup fees, monthly office recovery plans, event recovery packages, equipment kits, and grant-supported route development. The recommended entity path is a California benefit corporation pursuing B Corp certification, with nonprofit recipient partnerships and possible fiscal sponsorship if charitable grant funding becomes important.

Our first pilot is Oakland-first: one to three donor companies, one to two caterers, two to four recipient partners, and five to ten pickups. We will measure pounds recovered, recipient usefulness, donor friction, pickup success rate, and repeat willingness.

We are looking for pilot companies, caterers, recipient partners, advisors, and funders who want to make food recovery easier than throwing good food away.
