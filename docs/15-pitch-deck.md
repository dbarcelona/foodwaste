# Pitch Deck: Food Recovery Oakland

## Slide 1: Share your food. Keep neighbors fed.

Oakland-first food recovery for corporate catered surplus.

Speaker note:
Food Recovery Oakland helps companies recover safe surplus food from catered meetings and events and deliver it to local food access partners.

## Slide 2: The founder insight

Food waste was personal before it was strategic.

- learned at home not to waste a single grain of rice
- saw food insecurity growing up in NYC
- volunteered at food pantries
- watched waste continue in Berkeley, Oakland, and corporate event settings

Speaker note:
This company starts from a simple contradiction: we have enough food in the room, but not enough systems to move it to people.

## Slide 3: The problem

Catered food is wasted because the handoff is unclear.

- event teams are busy
- caterers are breaking down
- recipient organizations need predictability
- donors worry about safety and liability
- no one owns the documentation

Speaker note:
The problem is not a lack of goodwill. It is a lack of operational ownership.

## Slide 4: The solution

A recovery service for corporate catered surplus.

Workflow:

1. Screen the donation.
2. Match a recipient.
3. Pick up food.
4. Deliver directly.
5. Confirm receipt.
6. Report impact.

Speaker note:
We make donation easier than disposal.

## Slide 5: The wedge

Start with corporate events, not restaurants.

- higher-volume surplus
- clearer budget owner
- stronger ESG and employee engagement value
- repeatable office/event patterns
- less crowded than consumer restaurant discount apps

Speaker note:
The wedge is a B2B operations and reporting service, not a consumer marketplace.

## Slide 6: Why companies say yes

Companies get:

- less visible waste
- local community impact
- employee morale
- sustainability reporting
- a repeatable process
- reduced event-team burden

Speaker note:
The moral case matters, but the operational case closes the deal.

## Slide 7: Business model

Revenue hypotheses:

- $150 single pickup
- $750 launch pilot
- $1,500+ event recovery package
- monthly office plans
- equipment kits
- grant-supported route infrastructure

Speaker note:
The first pilot tests willingness to pay and operational cost per successful pickup.

## Slide 8: Partner ecosystem

Potential partners include:

- Alameda County Community Food Bank
- East Oakland Collective
- St. Vincent de Paul of Alameda County
- CityTeam Oakland
- Berkeley Food Network
- local caterers and venues

Speaker note:
We are not trying to replace food access organizations. We are trying to make the corporate donor side usable for them.

## Slide 9: Competition

Comparable models:

- Replate
- Copia
- Food Runners / ExtraFood
- White Pony Express
- Waste No Food
- Food Recovery Network

Differentiation:

- Oakland-first
- corporate catering focus
- readiness kits
- high-touch local operations
- donor-side reporting and repeatability

## Slide 10: Pilot plan

First 90 days:

- 1 to 3 donor companies
- 1 to 2 caterers
- 2 to 4 recipient partners
- 5 to 10 pickups
- no overnight storage
- direct delivery only
- written agreements and logs

Success:

- repeat donor interest
- recipient usefulness
- safe execution
- clear cost per pickup

## Slide 11: Entity path

Recommended:

- California benefit corporation
- B Corp-aligned policies from day one
- nonprofit recipient partners
- possible fiscal sponsor for charitable grants

Speaker note:
This keeps the company compatible with paid services and impact investment while still centering community benefit.

## Slide 12: The ask

We are looking for:

- pilot companies
- caterer partners
- recipient partners
- food safety/legal advisors
- insurance guidance
- funders or sponsors for the first route

Speaker note:
The next milestone is not scale. It is five excellent pickups that prove trust.
