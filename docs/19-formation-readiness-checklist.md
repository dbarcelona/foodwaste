# Formation Readiness Checklist

This checklist prepares you for attorney/CPA review. It is not legal or tax advice.

## Recommended first filing path

Default recommendation for the current model:

1. Form a California benefit corporation or another attorney-recommended for-profit social enterprise structure.
2. Build operations and governance around B Corp standards from day one.
3. Use nonprofit recipient partners for charitable distribution.
4. Use fiscal sponsorship only if charitable grants or tax-deductible donations become important before nonprofit formation.

## Before meeting an attorney

Prepare:

- founder legal name and address
- working company name options
- intended business activities
- intended public benefit purpose
- ownership expectations
- whether outside investment is likely
- whether cofounders may join
- expected revenue streams
- whether volunteers may be used
- states/cities where operations may occur
- insurance requirements from prospective corporate customers
- draft donor and recipient agreement term sheets

## Benefit corporation formation packet

Ask counsel to help prepare or review:

- name availability search
- Articles of Incorporation
- benefit corporation language
- bylaws
- initial board approvals
- founder stock issuance
- intellectual property assignment
- conflict-of-interest policy
- public benefit reporting plan
- EIN application
- California tax registration
- local business license requirements
- insurance package

## Nonprofit alternative packet

If the strategy shifts toward a charitable nonprofit, prepare:

- charitable purpose statement
- initial board slate
- board independence plan
- conflict-of-interest policy
- bylaws
- California nonprofit public benefit corporation Articles
- IRS Form 1023 or 1023-EZ eligibility review
- California FTB exemption path
- Attorney General charitable registration requirements
- donor receipt process
- grant accounting process

## Fiscal sponsorship screening questions

Ask potential fiscal sponsors:

- Do you sponsor food recovery or food access projects?
- Can sponsored projects conduct pickups?
- Can sponsored projects charge companies for services?
- Who signs donor and recipient agreements?
- Does your insurance cover food recovery operations?
- What fee do you charge?
- Who owns funds, equipment, brand assets, and data?
- Can you issue donation receipts for funds supporting this project?
- What reports do you require?

## First filing decision test

Choose benefit corporation first if:

- companies are willing to pay
- venture or impact investment is plausible
- logistics/reporting productization matters
- B Corp certification is a goal
- charitable receipts are not central to the first pilot

Choose nonprofit/fiscal sponsorship first if:

- grants and donations are the main funding source
- volunteers drive the model
- donors require tax-deductible receipts
- distribution to people in need is the direct operating activity

## Do not file until these are clear

- who owns the organization
- whether investors are expected
- whether charitable receipts are needed
- who bears food safety risk
- what insurance is available
- whether volunteers can legally and practically support operations
- whether recipient partners can accept prepared food
