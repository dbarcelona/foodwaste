# Grant Application Draft

## Project title

Oakland Corporate Catering Food Recovery Pilot

## Applicant

To be determined after entity/fiscal sponsorship decision.

## Project summary

The Oakland Corporate Catering Food Recovery Pilot will recover eligible surplus food from catered corporate events and office meals and deliver it to local food access organizations. The project will test a repeatable donor-side workflow that includes intake, food safety screening, pickup, recipient matching, delivery confirmation, and impact reporting.

## Need statement

Oakland and the broader Bay Area experience both food insecurity and routine edible food waste. Corporate catered events can produce surplus prepared food, but recovery often fails because no party owns the handoff. Event teams are busy, caterers are breaking down, recipient organizations need predictability, and donors worry about safety, logistics, and documentation.

## Project activities

- recruit pilot donor companies and caterers
- interview recipient organizations
- create accepted-food and refusal policies
- purchase pilot equipment
- complete controlled pickup and delivery routes
- collect food quantity, timing, and recipient usefulness data
- publish a pilot impact report

## Expected outcomes

- 5 to 10 completed pickups
- 500+ pounds recovered or a validated baseline
- 2 to 4 recipient partners engaged
- improved donor understanding of food recovery requirements
- documented workflow for recurring corporate food recovery

## Budget categories

- insulated food transport supplies
- thermometers and labels
- packaging
- driver mileage and labor
- insurance
- legal review
- data/reporting tools
- outreach materials
- fiscal sponsor fee if applicable

## Evaluation

The project will track:

- pickups completed
- pounds recovered
- food refused
- recipient usable-food rate
- route time
- cost per pickup
- donor satisfaction
- recipient satisfaction
- repeat pilot interest

## Sustainability

The pilot is designed to test a blended funding model. Ongoing operations may be supported through company-paid pickup fees, monthly recovery plans, event recovery packages, equipment/readiness kits, sponsorships, and grants for route infrastructure.

## Equity and community benefit

The project starts in Oakland and prioritizes local food access partners. Recipient organizations will be interviewed before receiving food so the service supports existing community infrastructure rather than creating burdensome or unwanted donations.
